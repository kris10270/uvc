Inactivation vs. UV Dose

How much 222nm dose does a pathogen need to absorb for meaningful inactivation? The dashed line shows the dose from the current plume transit.

Plume physics model: For cough/sneeze (impulsive puffs): turbulent jet centerline velocity decays as v(x) = V₀·(d₀/x) for x > d₀, where d₀ ≈ 2cm (effective mouth diameter). Transit time = ∫dx/v(x) = (d² - d₀²)/(2·V₀·d₀), capped at d/V₀ minimum. For breath/talking (sustained jets): approximately constant velocity, so transit ≈ d/V₀.

Survival model: Biphasic inactivation from guv-calcs: S(dose) = (1-f)·exp(-k1·dose) + f·exp(-k2·dose) where dose is in mJ/cm², k in cm²/mJ, and f is the resistant fraction.

UV dose: dose = irradiance(µW/cm²) × transit_time(s) / 1000 (converting µJ to mJ).
Pathogen susceptibility data: UVC Inactivation Constants database from guv-calcs (J. V. Belenky). Aerosol measurements preferred where available. Plume velocities from Gupta et al. (2009), Bourouiba et al. (2014), Abkarian et al. (2020). Built by Claude Opus 4.6 · v1.0 · GitHub.
